# Product Positioning Framework

Positioning is the place your product occupies in the customer's mind relative to alternatives. It is not what you say in an ad - it is how customers perceive you compared to everything else they could choose.

## April Dunford's Positioning Framework (Obviously Awesome)

The most rigorous modern framework for product positioning:

1. **Competitive alternatives**: What would customers do if your product did not exist? (Not "competitors" but actual alternatives including spreadsheets, manual processes, doing nothing)
2. **Unique attributes**: What does your product have that the alternatives do not?
3. **Value (not features)**: What value do those unique attributes enable? (Translate features into outcomes)
4. **Target customers**: Which customers care most about that value?
5. **Market frame of reference**: What category positions your value most favorably?

## Positioning Statement Format

For [target customer] who [has a specific need or problem], [product name] is a [market category] that [key benefit]. Unlike [primary alternative], our product [primary differentiator].

Example: For engineering managers at Series A-C startups who struggle to maintain visibility on distributed team work, Acme is a lightweight async update tool that surfaces what matters without adding meetings. Unlike Jira, it is designed for communication, not project tracking.

## Messaging Framework

A messaging framework translates positioning into layered messages for different audiences:

- **Tagline**: 5-7 words. What you do for whom.
- **Elevator pitch**: 2 sentences. Problem, solution, unique angle.
- **Hero section copy**: Headline (benefit) + subheadline (how) + CTA
- **Sales 30-second pitch**: Problem, why current alternatives fail, our solution, result
- **Customer testimonial angle**: What outcome should your ideal testimonial showcase?
