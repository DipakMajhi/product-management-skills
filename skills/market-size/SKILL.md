# Market Size

This skill routes to **market-sizing**, which provides a complete market sizing framework using three complementary approaches.

**When to use this:**
- Building a business case or investor pitch that requires market size
- Stress-testing a market opportunity before committing to a strategy
- Answering "how big is this market?" in PM or founder interviews
- Sizing a new market entry before a go-to-market plan

**Quick methodology guide:**
- Large established market with available industry data → Top-down (TAM from analyst reports, SAM by segment filter)
- New or niche market with no published data → Bottom-up (count of potential customers × value per customer)
- Sanity-checking a size estimate → Fermi estimation (first-principles decomposition)
- Presenting to investors or execs → Use all three and triangulate; show the range, not just one number

**What market-sizing covers:**
- TAM / SAM / SOM hierarchy — definitions, common mistakes, and how each is used
- Top-down methodology: industry report sourcing, segment filtering, adjusting for geography and use case
- Bottom-up methodology: customer count × ACV, building from unit economics
- Fermi estimation: breaking any market into components you can estimate independently
- Confidence ranges — presenting market sizes with honest error bars, not false precision
- Common market sizing errors: double-counting, confusing addressable with realistic, using TAM as pipeline
- Market growth rate analysis — how to incorporate CAGR and assess timing of the opportunity
- Comparable company analysis: using public company revenue as a market proxy
- Market sizing for investor decks vs. internal strategy documents — different depths required

**The skill will:**
1. Ask clarifying questions: what is your product, who are the customers, what geography, what time horizon
2. Run a top-down estimate using available market data
3. Run a bottom-up estimate from first principles
4. Cross-check with Fermi decomposition
5. Triangulate the three estimates and explain the range
6. Apply market growth rate to project 3-5 year opportunity
7. Flag assumptions that would most significantly change the estimate
