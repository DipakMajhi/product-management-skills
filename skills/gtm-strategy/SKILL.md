# Go-to-Market Strategy Builder

A go-to-market strategy defines how you will reach your target customers and deliver your value proposition to generate demand and drive adoption.

## GTM Strategy Components

Apply this skill to: $ARGUMENTS


### 1. Beachhead Segment
The specific, narrow customer segment you will focus on first. The beachhead is not your total addressable market - it is the smallest segment where you can win decisively, and from which you can expand.

Properties of a good beachhead:
- Small enough to dominate (you can reach most of them)
- Large enough to matter (revenue potential justifies the focus)
- High pain (they desperately need what you offer)
- Accessible (you can reach them through specific channels)
- Reference-able (a win here opens doors to the next segment)

### 2. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
The fictional perfect customer in your beachhead segment. ICP defines:
- Company firmographics (for B2B): size, industry, funding stage, tech stack, geography
- Buyer role: who has budget and authority
- Champion role: who will advocate internally
- User role: who uses the product daily
- Trigger events: what causes them to start looking for a solution

### 3. Messaging and Positioning
The core messages that will resonate with the beachhead. (Use the positioning skill for depth.)

### 4. GTM Motion
How you will reach and convert customers:
- **Product-Led Growth (PLG)**: Product is the primary acquisition and expansion driver (self-serve, freemium, viral loops)
- **Sales-Led Growth (SLG)**: Sales team drives acquisition (outbound, inbound follow-up, enterprise deals)
- **Marketing-Led Growth**: Content, brand, and demand generation drive inbound
- **Partner-Led Growth**: Channel partners, integrations, or marketplaces drive distribution

Most products use a combination. Define the primary motion and supporting motions.

### 5. Launch Plan
- Pre-launch (6-8 weeks before): ICP list, beta users, messaging validation, content preparation
- Launch week: Press/PR, launch channels, sales enablement, support preparation
- Post-launch (4 weeks): Performance review, feedback loops, iteration plan

### 6. Launch Success Metrics
Define success clearly before launch:
- Acquisition metric (signups, trials, leads)
- Activation metric (% of new users who reach first value)
- Revenue metric (MRR or deal count from launch cohort)
- Feedback metric (NPS or qualitative themes from first 30 days)
