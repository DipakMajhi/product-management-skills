# North Star

This skill routes to **metrics-and-okrs** Mode A (North Star Metric), which provides a complete North Star definition methodology.

**When to use this:**
- Your team has no agreed single most-important metric and debates priorities constantly
- Your current North Star is a vanity metric (DAU, downloads, page views) that doesn't track real value
- Launching a new product and need to anchor metrics before writing OKRs
- Answering "what's the North Star Metric for this product?" in PM interviews

**Quick guide by business type:**
- Marketplace / transaction product → A metric that captures both sides: GMV, completed transactions, repeat buyers
- SaaS / productivity tool → A metric that captures work done: active projects, documents created, tasks completed
- Consumer social / content → A metric that captures genuine engagement: content consumed per user, meaningful interactions
- B2B enterprise → A metric that predicts expansion revenue: seats used, workflows automated, data processed
- Two-sided network → Consider a combined metric that captures cross-side interaction, not just one side

**What metrics-and-okrs Mode A covers:**
- North Star Metric definition: the single metric that best captures the value your product delivers to users
- Business game classification: attention, transaction, productivity, transformation — each suggests a different NSM type
- 5-point NSM quality test: captures user value, predicts retention, guides trade-offs, is measurable, is actionable
- Input metrics: 3-5 leading indicators that drive the NSM (the levers your team controls directly)
- Guardrail metrics: what must not degrade when you optimize the NSM
- NSM anti-patterns: vanity metrics, revenue-only metrics, activity metrics divorced from value
- North Star vs. business metric — why they're different and why you need both
- Connecting NSM to OKRs: how the North Star becomes the basis for quarterly targets

**The skill will:**
1. Ask about your product's core value exchange and user behavior
2. Classify your business game to guide the metric search
3. Propose 2-3 NSM candidates and evaluate each against the 5-point test
4. Select the best NSM with explicit reasoning
5. Define 3-5 input metrics that are the levers to move the NSM
6. Define guardrail metrics
7. Show how to translate the NSM into a quarterly OKR target
