# Retention Analysis Framework

Retention is the foundation of sustainable growth. Acquiring users who do not stay is filling a leaky bucket. Fixing retention compounds - every improvement affects every future cohort.

## Retention Diagnostic Framework

Apply this skill to: $ARGUMENTS


### Step 1: Define Retention Precisely
"Retention" means different things in different products. Define:
- What action constitutes "retained"? (Logged in? Performed a core action? Paid their renewal?)
- Over what time window? (Day 1, Day 7, Day 30, Month 3?)
- For which segment? (All users? Activated users? Paid users?)

### Step 2: Identify the Retention Curve Shape

**High initial drop, stable tail**: Classic. Most users leave in week 1; those who stay become habits.
Diagnosis: Activation problem. Fix onboarding.

**Gradual decline that does not flatten**: Users are leaving slowly but continuously.
Diagnosis: Habit formation problem. Fix engagement loops, notifications, or product depth.

**Improving then declining**: Users come back after a strong initial experience but stop returning.
Diagnosis: Lack of sustained value delivery. Fix expansion features or workflow integration.

**Step-function drops**: A specific event causes a mass departure (price change, feature removal, competitor launch).
Diagnosis: External shock. Investigate the event and design a response.

### Step 3: Find the "Activation Moment"
The activation moment is the first experience that makes a new user realize the product's core value. Users who reach this moment retain dramatically better than those who do not.

To find it: Analyze what Day 30 retained users did in their first week that churned users did not.

### Step 4: Map the Retention Journey
Where exactly are users falling off? Build a funnel from:
Signup to Profile complete to First core action to Second core action to Invite/share to Day 7 return

### Step 5: Design Retention Interventions

| Problem | Intervention |
|---|---|
| Users do not understand the product | Improve onboarding, empty states, and first-run experience |
| Users understand but do not form a habit | Add triggers (push, email, in-app nudges), build streaks or progress |
| Users have not completed setup | Progressive onboarding that drives toward the activation moment |
| Users are not using key retention-driving features | Feature discovery: tooltips, in-app announcements, proactive tips |
| Users who were retained are now at risk | Early warning system using predictive churn signals |
