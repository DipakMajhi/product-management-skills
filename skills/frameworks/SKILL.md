# Frameworks

This skill routes to **strategic-frameworks**, which provides 12 strategic analysis frameworks with selection guidance and worked applications.

**When to use this:**
- Conducting a structured analysis of a market, competitive position, or strategic decision
- Preparing a strategy review, board presentation, or investor update
- Running a structured workshop on company or product direction
- Answering "walk me through a strategic analysis of X" in PM or leadership interviews

**Quick framework selection guide:**
- Need a general-purpose starting point → SWOT/TOWS: strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats
- Analyzing industry attractiveness → Porter's Five Forces: supplier power, buyer power, substitutes, new entrants, rivalry
- Scanning the macro environment → PESTLE: political, economic, social, technological, legal, environmental
- Deciding where to grow next → Ansoff Matrix: market penetration / product development / market development / diversification
- Diagnosing a strategic problem → Rumelt's Good Strategy Kernel: diagnosis, guiding policy, coherent actions
- Choosing a competitive position → Porter's Generic Strategies: cost leadership / differentiation / focus
- Building a strategy → Playing to Win (Lafley & Martin): winning aspiration, where to play, how to win, capabilities, management systems
- Allocating across time horizons → McKinsey Three Horizons: core / adjacent / transformational
- Mapping strategic groups → Strategic Group Analysis
- Platform and ecosystem strategy → Platform Strategy Canvas
- Jobs-to-be-Done innovation → Christensen's Disruptive Innovation lens
- Value chain analysis → Porter's Value Chain

**What strategic-frameworks covers:**
- All 12 frameworks with full methodology, when to use, worked example, and output template
- Framework combinations: when and how to run SWOT + Porter's Five Forces together
- TOWS matrix: converting SWOT insights into strategic options (SO, ST, WO, WT strategies)
- Facilitation guide: how to run each framework as a team workshop
- Output templates for executive presentation
- Anti-patterns: misapplying frameworks, framework theater vs. real insight, treating frameworks as answers rather than tools

**The skill will:**
1. Ask about the context — what decision or question are you trying to answer?
2. Recommend the most appropriate framework or combination
3. Apply the framework to your specific context
4. Deliver structured outputs with actionable insights (not just filled-in boxes)
5. Identify the most critical implications for your product or strategy decision
