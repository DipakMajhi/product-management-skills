# Vision

This skill routes to **product-vision**, which provides a complete product vision crafting framework.

**When to use this:**
- Writing or rewriting a product vision from scratch
- Testing whether your existing vision is strong enough to guide trade-offs
- Preparing a vision document for a board, exec team, or new team member
- Answering "where do you want to take this product in 5 years?" in PM interviews

**Quick format guide:**
- Internal team alignment → Narrative vision (2-3 paragraph future user story)
- Board, exec, or investor communication → One-sentence vision
- Major new product or pivot → Amazon Working Backwards Press Release/FAQ
- Structured planning artifact → Roman Pichler's Product Vision Board

**What product-vision covers:**
- 7-point vision quality test (specific, ambitious, human-centered, time-bounded, memorable, falsifiable, differentiated)
- Vision vs. mission vs. strategy vs. goals — distinctions and cascade
- Narrative vision format with worked example
- One-sentence vision template: "[User] can [do what] so that [outcome]"
- Press Release/FAQ format (Amazon Working Backwards) — full 7-section structure
- Roman Pichler's Product Vision Board
- Jeff Bezos backward-from-the-future thinking technique
- Anti-patterns: generic aspiration, feature list, financial target, buzzword soup — with examples
- Vision testing: using the vision to make hard trade-offs
- Team alignment and stakeholder buy-in techniques

**The skill will:**
1. Ask for your product, primary user, and aspirational future state
2. Evaluate any existing vision language against the 7-point quality test
3. Recommend the right format for your context
4. Craft the vision in your chosen format with a worked example
5. Flag any anti-patterns in your draft and suggest precise corrections
6. Show how the vision cascades to a strategy statement
