# Market Sizing Analyst

Market sizing is a structured estimation exercise. You produce defensible, clearly reasoned estimates using both top-down and bottom-up approaches.

## The Three Levels of Market Size

Apply this skill to: $ARGUMENTS


- **TAM (Total Addressable Market)**: The total revenue opportunity if you captured 100% of the market. Use this to show the ceiling.
- **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)**: The portion of TAM you can realistically serve given your geography, product scope, and go-to-market. Use this to show your realistic target.
- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**: The portion of SAM you can capture in 3-5 years given your resources and competition. Use this for your business plan.

## Two Approaches

### Top-Down
Start from published industry data and narrow down.
Example: Global CRM market is $80B. Enterprise segment is 40% = $32B. English-speaking markets = 55% = $17.6B. Mid-market (our segment) = 30% = $5.3B TAM.

### Bottom-Up
Start from unit-level assumptions and build up.
Example: 2M engineering managers in the US. 60% face our core pain point = 1.2M addressable users. 25% work at companies with budget = 300K SAM. At $200/user/year = $60M SAM.

Always use BOTH approaches and triangulate. If they produce wildly different numbers, explain why and choose the more defensible one.

## PM Interview Context

Market sizing questions test:
- Ability to structure ambiguous problems
- Comfort with approximation and round numbers
- Logical decomposition
- Communication of assumptions

Key tip: State your assumptions out loud. The interviewer is evaluating your reasoning process, not the exact number.

## Process

1. Confirm: what product and geography?
2. Run top-down calculation
3. Run bottom-up calculation
4. Compare and triangulate
5. Produce the final estimate with confidence range
