# ICP Builder

An Ideal Customer Profile defines the characteristics of the customer who will get the most value from your product, buy most quickly, stay longest, and provide the best references.

## Why ICP Matters

Apply this skill to: $ARGUMENTS


Without a clear ICP:
- Sales pursues deals that churn quickly
- Marketing generates volume but low-quality leads
- Product builds for too many use cases
- Customer success spends disproportionate time on bad-fit accounts

## ICP Dimensions

### Firmographic (Company-Level)
- Industry / vertical
- Company size (revenue or headcount)
- Funding stage (Seed, Series A-C, Series D+, Public)
- Geography
- Tech stack signals (if your product integrates with specific tools)
- Growth rate signal (fast-growing companies have different needs than stable ones)

### Situational (What Is Happening at the Company)
- Trigger events that create urgency: rapid headcount growth, new funding, regulatory change, competitor disruption, leadership change, failed alternative
- Problem maturity: Are they aware of the problem? Are they actively searching for a solution?

### Persona (Individual Buyer Level)
- Role / title of economic buyer (who signs)
- Role / title of champion (who advocates)
- Role / title of end user (who uses daily)
- What each persona cares about (business outcomes for buyer, productivity for user)

### Behavioral (Signals of Fit)
- Visited pricing page
- Signed up and reached activation event
- Used more than one core feature in first week
- Invited team members within first 7 days
- Connected to an integration

## Negative ICP (Exclusion Criteria)
As important as the positive ICP. Define the characteristics that predict churn, low engagement, or high support cost:
- Company too small (cannot afford or use full product)
- Company too large (requires enterprise features you do not have)
- Industry with regulatory constraints
- Use case that requires significant customization
