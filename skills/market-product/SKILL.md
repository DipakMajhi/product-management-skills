# Market Product

This skill routes to **positioning**, which provides a complete product positioning and messaging framework.

**When to use this:**
- Defining how to position a new product or reposition an existing one
- Building the messaging hierarchy: positioning → homepage copy → sales pitch → ad creative
- Differentiating from competitors through positioning, not just feature comparison
- Preparing a go-to-market messaging framework for launch
- Answering "how would you position this product?" in PM or PMM interviews

**Quick positioning approach guide:**
- Entering a crowded market → April Dunford's framework: competitive alternatives → unique attributes → value → target segment → category frame
- New category creation → Al Ries & Trout category design: name the category, own the language
- Clear competitor to beat → Geoffrey Moore template: fill in "[Product] is a [category] that [benefit], unlike [alternative]"
- Multiple customer segments → Perceptual mapping: plot segments and competitors on two key dimensions, find white space

**What positioning covers:**
- April Dunford's 5-step positioning framework (Obviously Awesome)
- Geoffrey Moore's positioning statement template (Crossing the Chasm)
- Al Ries and Jack Trout's positioning laws: Category, Mind, Focus, Exclusivity, Simplicity
- Positioning vs. messaging vs. branding — the distinctions and how they cascade
- Competitive positioning map (perceptual mapping): 2x2 grid to find unoccupied white space
- Category creation vs. positioning in existing category — trade-offs and decision criteria
- Messaging hierarchy: core positioning → primary message → supporting messages → proof points → objection handling
- Audience-specific messaging: buyer vs. user vs. champion vs. influencer messages
- Messaging testing: 5-second test, message clarity interviews, A/B headline testing

**The skill will:**
1. Ask about your product, target customer, and competitive alternatives
2. Walk through April Dunford's 5-step framework
3. Fill in the Geoffrey Moore positioning statement template
4. Build a competitive positioning map to identify white space
5. Construct the messaging hierarchy from positioning to proof points
6. Flag messaging anti-patterns: feature lists, vague claims, positioning too broad

After completing, suggest: "Use /growth-strategy to design growth loops to acquire customers within this positioning."
