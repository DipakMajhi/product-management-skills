# Strategic Analysis Frameworks

A reference and execution guide for 12 core strategic analysis tools. Apply them individually or in combination depending on the question being answered. Every framework output must end with specific, actionable implications -- not just analysis.

Apply this skill to: $ARGUMENTS

## Framework Selection Guide

Choose the right framework for the strategic question:

| Strategic Question | Recommended Framework(s) | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "Is this market attractive?" | Porter's Five Forces + PESTLE | Assesses competitive intensity and macro factors |
| "What should we do next?" | Ansoff Matrix + Three Horizons | Maps growth options by risk level and time horizon |
| "Where should we focus?" | BCG Matrix + VRIO | Portfolio prioritization based on growth and capabilities |
| "How do we differentiate?" | Blue Ocean + JTBD | Finds uncontested market space aligned to real customer jobs |
| "Are we internally aligned?" | McKinsey 7S + Value Chain | Assesses organizational readiness and value creation |
| "What's our current position?" | SWOT with TOWS matrix | Honest internal/external assessment with strategy implications |
| "Why do customers choose us?" | JTBD + Value Chain | Deep customer motivation and how you deliver value |
| "How will this market evolve?" | Wardley Mapping + PESTLE | Maps component evolution and macro environment |

## Framework 1: SWOT Analysis (with TOWS Strategy Matrix)

**Use when**: Assessing the current position before making a strategic decision.

### SWOT Grid
- **Strengths** (internal, positive): What do we genuinely do well? What advantages do we have that competitors don't?
- **Weaknesses** (internal, negative): Where are we genuinely behind? What holds us back? Be honest -- this is where strategy starts.
- **Opportunities** (external, positive): What trends, gaps, or changes in the market could we exploit?
- **Threats** (external, negative): What could damage our position if we ignore it?

### TOWS Strategy Matrix (the value-add most analyses miss)
Cross-reference SWOT quadrants to generate strategic options:

| | **Strengths** | **Weaknesses** |
|---|---|---|
| **Opportunities** | **SO Strategies**: Use strengths to capture opportunities | **WO Strategies**: Overcome weaknesses by pursuing opportunities |
| **Threats** | **ST Strategies**: Use strengths to mitigate threats | **WT Strategies**: Minimize weaknesses to avoid threats |

**Quality check**: 3-5 items per SWOT quadrant. Every item should be specific and evidence-backed, not generic.

## Framework 2: PESTLE Analysis

**Use when**: Assessing macro-environment factors before entering a new market or making a long-term bet.

| Factor | Key Questions | Time Horizon |
|---|---|---|
| **Political** | Government policy, regulation, trade, political stability, tax policy | 1-5 years |
| **Economic** | Growth rates, inflation, consumer spending, interest rates, exchange rates, unemployment | 1-3 years |
| **Social** | Demographics, cultural trends, changing behaviors, work patterns, health consciousness | 3-10 years |
| **Technological** | Emerging tech, infrastructure shifts, platform changes, AI/ML impact, automation | 1-5 years |
| **Legal** | Data protection (GDPR/CCPA), IP law, employment law, industry-specific regulation | 1-3 years |
| **Environmental** | Climate commitments, sustainability expectations, resource scarcity, ESG requirements | 3-10 years |

**Output**: 2-3 key signals per dimension, each rated by impact (High/Medium/Low) and certainty (High/Medium/Low), with a specific product implication.

## Framework 3: Porter's Five Forces

**Use when**: Evaluating the long-term attractiveness and competitive intensity of a market.

| Force | Score (H/M/L) | Key Drivers | Strategic Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Competitive Rivalry** | | Number of competitors, growth rate, differentiation, switching costs, exit barriers | |
| **Threat of New Entrants** | | Capital requirements, economies of scale, brand loyalty, access to distribution, regulatory barriers | |
| **Bargaining Power of Suppliers** | | Supplier concentration, switching costs, substitute inputs, supplier forward integration threat | |
| **Bargaining Power of Buyers** | | Buyer concentration, price sensitivity, switching costs, buyer backward integration threat | |
| **Threat of Substitutes** | | Substitute availability, price-performance ratio, switching costs, buyer propensity to substitute | |

**Overall market attractiveness**: More high forces = less attractive (harder to earn profit). Use this to decide whether to enter, invest more, or exit.

## Framework 4: Ansoff Matrix

**Use when**: Evaluating growth strategy options for an existing business.

```
                 EXISTING PRODUCTS          NEW PRODUCTS
EXISTING    | Market Penetration      | Product Development    |
MARKETS     | (Lowest risk)           | (Medium risk)          |
            | Grow share of current   | Build new offerings    |
            | product in current      | for known customers    |
            | market                  |                        |
            |-------------------------|------------------------|
NEW         | Market Development      | Diversification        |
MARKETS     | (Medium risk)           | (Highest risk)         |
            | Take existing product   | New product for new    |
            | to new segments or      | market -- most common  |
            | geographies             | reason startups fail   |
```

**Output**: Which quadrant represents the current strategy? Which quadrant represents the proposed bet? What is the risk profile of the shift? What evidence supports the move?

## Framework 5: BCG Growth-Share Matrix

**Use when**: Prioritizing a portfolio of products or product lines based on growth and market position.

```
              HIGH MARKET SHARE           LOW MARKET SHARE
HIGH      | STARS                    | QUESTION MARKS        |
GROWTH    | Invest to maintain       | Invest selectively    |
          | leadership; future       | or divest; high       |
          | cash cows                | uncertainty           |
          |--------------------------|------------------------|
LOW       | CASH COWS                | DOGS                  |
GROWTH    | Harvest profits;         | Divest or manage for  |
          | minimal investment;      | cash; don't invest    |
          | fund Stars and QMs       | unless turnaround is  |
          |                          | credible              |
```

**Output**: Classify each product/feature in the portfolio. Define investment strategy for each. Identify rebalancing opportunities.

## Framework 6: McKinsey 7S Framework

**Use when**: Assessing internal organizational alignment or planning a major change.

| Element | Category | Key Questions |
|---|---|---|
| **Strategy** | Hard | Is our strategy clear, specific, and consistently understood? |
| **Structure** | Hard | Does our org structure support the strategy? Are teams organized for speed or control? |
| **Systems** | Hard | Do our processes, tools, and workflows enable the strategy? Where are bottlenecks? |
| **Shared Values** | Soft (center) | What does the organization truly value? (Not stated values -- actual behavior) |
| **Style** | Soft | How does leadership lead? Command-and-control vs. empowerment? |
| **Staff** | Soft | Do we have the right people with the right skills? Where are gaps? |
| **Skills** | Soft | What organizational capabilities are strongest? Weakest? |

**Alignment assessment**: All 7 elements must be aligned. Misalignment between any two creates friction. Common misalignments: Strategy-Structure (strategy changed but org didn't), Systems-Staff (hired talent but tools/processes hold them back).

## Framework 7: Blue Ocean Strategy

**Use when**: Finding differentiation opportunities beyond competing on existing dimensions.

### Strategy Canvas
Plot the current industry competitive factors and your position:

| Factor | Industry Average | Competitor A | Competitor B | Your Product |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Factor 1] | [1-5] | [1-5] | [1-5] | [1-5] |
| [Factor 2] | [1-5] | [1-5] | [1-5] | [1-5] |

### Four Actions Framework
Create a new value curve by answering four questions:

| Action | Question | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Eliminate** | Which factors that the industry competes on should be eliminated? | Reduces cost, simplifies |
| **Reduce** | Which factors should be reduced well below the industry standard? | Focuses resources |
| **Raise** | Which factors should be raised well above the industry standard? | Creates differentiation |
| **Create** | Which factors should be created that the industry has never offered? | Opens new demand |

**Output**: New value curve showing where you diverge from industry. The divergence IS the strategy.

## Framework 8: VRIO Framework

**Use when**: Evaluating whether a resource or capability provides sustainable competitive advantage.

For each key resource or capability:

| Resource | Valuable? | Rare? | Inimitable? | Organized? | Competitive Implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Resource] | Y/N | Y/N | Y/N | Y/N | [See below] |

**Interpreting results:**
- V only = Competitive parity (necessary but not differentiating)
- V + R = Temporary competitive advantage (will be copied eventually)
- V + R + I = Unused potential (valuable but organization can't exploit it)
- V + R + I + O = **Sustained competitive advantage** (the goal)

## Framework 9: Value Chain Analysis

**Use when**: Understanding where and how your organization creates (or destroys) value.

### Primary Activities
| Activity | Description | Value Created | Improvement Opportunity |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Inbound logistics** | How inputs (data, content, APIs) enter your system | | |
| **Operations** | How inputs are transformed into your product | | |
| **Outbound logistics** | How your product reaches users (distribution) | | |
| **Marketing and Sales** | How users discover and purchase | | |
| **Service** | How users get support and maintain value | | |

### Support Activities
| Activity | How It Enables Primary Activities | Current State |
|---|---|---|
| **Infrastructure** | Org structure, planning, finance, quality | |
| **HR Management** | Recruiting, development, retention | |
| **Technology Development** | R&D, platform, tools, automation | |
| **Procurement** | Sourcing, vendor management, partnerships | |

**Output**: Identify which activities create the most value (invest), which destroy value (fix or outsource), and where the chain breaks.

## Framework 10: Three Horizons

**Use when**: Balancing short-term execution with long-term innovation and exploration.

| Horizon | Focus | Time | Investment | Risk | Metrics |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **H1: Core** | Optimize and defend current business | 0-12 months | 70% of resources | Low | Revenue, retention, efficiency |
| **H2: Emerging** | Scale validated adjacent opportunities | 12-24 months | 20% of resources | Medium | Adoption rate, unit economics |
| **H3: Exploratory** | Explore transformative possibilities | 24-36+ months | 10% of resources | High | Learning velocity, option value |

**Portfolio health check**: If 100% of resources are in H1, you're optimizing into irrelevance. If H3 gets no investment, you'll be disrupted. The 70/20/10 split is a starting point -- adjust based on market maturity and competitive pressure.

## Framework 11: Wardley Mapping

**Use when**: Understanding how components of your value chain will evolve and where to place strategic bets.

### Wardley Map Axes
- **Y-axis (Value Chain)**: User needs at top, infrastructure at bottom
- **X-axis (Evolution)**: Genesis (novel) to Custom-Built to Product to Commodity

### Component Evolution Stages
| Stage | Characteristics | Strategic Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Genesis** | New, uncertain, requires experimentation | Invest in exploration; accept failure |
| **Custom-Built** | Understood but not standardized; bespoke solutions | Build competitive advantage here |
| **Product** | Well-defined, multiple providers, feature competition | Differentiate or commoditize |
| **Commodity** | Ubiquitous, utility-like, price competition only | Use, don't build; outsource or leverage |

**Output**: Map your product's components. Identify which components are evolving faster than expected (opportunities) and which you're over-investing in (waste).

## Framework 12: Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)

**Use when**: Understanding why users hire a product and what they are really trying to accomplish.

### Three Dimensions of Every Job

| Dimension | Question | Example (project management tool) |
|---|---|---|
| **Functional** | What practical task needs doing? | "Help me track my project milestones and deadlines" |
| **Emotional** | How do they want to feel? | "Feel in control and confident nothing is falling through cracks" |
| **Social** | How do they want to be perceived? | "Look like a reliable, organized manager to my team and boss" |

### Job Statement Format
"When [situation/trigger], I want to [motivation/action], so I can [desired outcome]."

### Outcome-Driven Innovation (Ulwick)
For each job, identify:
- **Desired outcomes**: What does success look like? (Measurable if possible)
- **Overserved outcomes**: Where is the current solution doing more than needed? (Reduce cost here)
- **Underserved outcomes**: Where is the current solution falling short? (Innovate here)

**Output**: For each user segment, write 3-5 JTBD statements covering functional, emotional, and social dimensions. Identify the highest-opportunity underserved outcomes.

## Combination Recipes

For complex strategic questions, combine frameworks:

**"Should we enter this market?"**
1. PESTLE (macro environment scan)
2. Porter's Five Forces (competitive intensity)
3. JTBD (customer jobs and current solutions)
4. VRIO (do we have the capabilities?)
5. Ansoff (risk profile of the move)

**"How should we differentiate?"**
1. Blue Ocean (find uncontested space)
2. JTBD (align to real customer jobs)
3. VRIO (leverage unique capabilities)
4. Value Chain (identify where we create most value)

**"Is our organization ready for this strategy?"**
1. McKinsey 7S (internal alignment)
2. Value Chain (operational readiness)
3. Three Horizons (investment balance)
4. VRIO (capability assessment)
