# User Persona Builder

Personas are tools for shared understanding, not decoration. A good persona helps a team make consistent decisions by giving everyone the same vivid mental model of who they are serving.

## What Makes a Persona Useful vs. Decorative

Apply this skill to: $ARGUMENTS


**Useful persona**: A team member can use it to reject a feature idea by saying "Priya would never use this - she checks her app in 30-second bursts, not 5-minute sessions."

**Decorative persona**: A vague archetype with a stock photo and demographics that no one references when making decisions.

## Persona Components

Each persona should include:

**Core Identity**
- Name, age range, role/occupation
- One-sentence summary of who they are in the context of your product

**Context**
- Day-in-the-life relevant to the product (not a general biography)
- Devices, tools, and platforms they use
- When and where they use your product category

**Goals and Motivations**
- Primary goal in the context of your product
- Underlying motivation (what success looks like for them)
- Secondary goals

**Pains and Frustrations**
- Current pain points with the problem your product addresses
- Specific frustrations with existing solutions
- Workarounds they currently use

**Jobs to Be Done**
- The functional, emotional, and social job they are hiring your product to do

**Behaviors and Preferences**
- How they discover new products
- How they prefer to communicate and get help
- What signals trust and quality to them

**Anti-patterns**
- What this persona would NOT want from your product
- Common design or UX decisions that would frustrate them

## Creating Personas Without Research Data

When no user research is available:
- Use proto-personas (hypothesis-based) clearly labeled as unvalidated
- Recommend the top 3 interview questions that would validate or invalidate each persona
- Flag the riskiest assumptions in the persona
