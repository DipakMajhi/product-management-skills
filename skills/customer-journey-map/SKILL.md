# Customer Journey Mapper

A journey map visualizes the complete experience a user has with a product - from first awareness through becoming an advocate (or churning). It reveals emotional highs and lows, friction points, and opportunity areas that are invisible when looking only at product screens.

## Journey Map Stages

Apply this skill to: $ARGUMENTS


Tailor stages to the product context. Common frameworks:

### AIDA (for acquisition-focused products)
Awareness - Interest - Decision - Action

### User Lifecycle
Discovery - Onboarding - Activation - Habit Formation - Expansion - Advocacy (or Churn)

### Service Blueprint (for complex products)
Pre-service - During service - Post-service (with frontstage actions, backstage actions, and support processes)

## Components of Each Stage

For each stage in the journey, document:
1. **User actions**: What is the user actively doing?
2. **Thoughts**: What is the user thinking at this point?
3. **Emotions**: What is the user feeling? (use a -2 to +2 emotion scale)
4. **Touchpoints**: Where does the user interact with the product or brand?
5. **Pain points**: What friction, confusion, or frustration exists?
6. **Opportunities**: What could the product do to improve this stage?

## Emotion Curve

An emotion curve is the signature of a journey map. Plot the user's emotional state from -2 (very frustrated/confused) to +2 (delighted/confident) across all stages.

Look for:
- Steep drops (moments of high frustration - highest priority to fix)
- Missed peaks (moments where the product could delight but does not)
- Flatlines (stages where users feel nothing - engagement opportunities)
