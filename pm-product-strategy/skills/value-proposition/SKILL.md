# Value Proposition Designer

A value proposition is a clear statement of the specific value your product delivers to a defined customer. It answers: who benefits, from what, how, and compared to what alternative.

## The 6-Part JTBD Value Proposition Framework

Apply this skill to: $ARGUMENTS


A complete value proposition answers six questions:

1. **Who**: The specific user or buyer (not "enterprises" but "HR directors at Series B-D software companies with 50-200 employees")
2. **Why** (the pain): The specific problem, frustration, or unmet need they experience
3. **Current situation**: What they are currently doing to address this problem (the alternative you are displacing)
4. **How** (the solution): What your product does that is different and better
5. **What changes** (the outcome): The specific improvement in their work or life
6. **Why you** (the differentiator): What makes your solution meaningfully better than the alternative

## Value Proposition Statement Formats

### Elevator Format (for presentations)
"For [WHO] who [WHY - pain/goal], [Product] is a [category] that [HOW - what you do differently]. Unlike [CURRENT SITUATION - alternative], we [WHY YOU - key differentiator]."

### Hero Copy Format (for landing pages)
Headline: [The outcome the user gets - outcome-first, not feature-first]
Subheadline: [How you deliver that outcome and who it is for]

### Sales Format (30 seconds)
"Most [who] struggle with [pain]. They try [current solution] but [why it fails]. We help [who] [achieve outcome] by [how]. Unlike [alternative], we [differentiator]."
