# Strategic Analysis Frameworks Reference

A reference for five core strategic analysis tools. Apply them individually or in combination depending on the question being answered.

## Framework 1: SWOT Analysis

Apply this skill to: $ARGUMENTS


**Use when**: Assessing the current position of a product, team, or company before making a strategic decision.

Structure:
- **Strengths** (internal, positive): What do we genuinely do well? What advantages do we have?
- **Weaknesses** (internal, negative): Where are we genuinely behind? What holds us back?
- **Opportunities** (external, positive): What trends or gaps in the market could we exploit?
- **Threats** (external, negative): What could damage our position if we ignore it?

Output: 3-5 items per quadrant, then a SO/ST/WO/WT strategy table mapping how to act on each combination.

## Framework 2: PESTLE Analysis

**Use when**: Assessing macro-environment factors before entering a new market or making a long-term bet.

Structure:
- **Political**: Government policy, regulation, trade, political stability
- **Economic**: Growth rates, inflation, consumer spending power, exchange rates
- **Social**: Demographics, cultural trends, changing user behaviors
- **Technological**: Emerging technologies, infrastructure shifts, platform changes
- **Legal**: Laws, intellectual property, data protection, employment rules
- **Environmental**: Climate, sustainability expectations, resource availability

Output: 2-3 key signals per dimension, each with an implication for the product.

## Framework 3: Porter's Five Forces

**Use when**: Evaluating the long-term attractiveness and competitive intensity of a market.

Structure:
- **Competitive rivalry**: How intense is competition among existing players?
- **Threat of new entrants**: How easy is it for new players to enter?
- **Bargaining power of suppliers**: How much leverage do key suppliers have?
- **Bargaining power of buyers**: How much leverage do customers have?
- **Threat of substitutes**: How easily can customers switch to a different solution type?

Score each force: High / Medium / Low, then assess overall market attractiveness.

## Framework 4: Ansoff Matrix

**Use when**: Evaluating growth strategy options for an existing business.

Four quadrants:
- **Market Penetration** (existing product, existing market): Grow share - lowest risk
- **Market Development** (existing product, new market): Expand to new segments or geographies
- **Product Development** (new product, existing market): Build new offerings for known customers
- **Diversification** (new product, new market): Highest risk, highest potential reward

Output: Which quadrant represents the current strategy? Which quadrant represents the proposed bet? What is the risk profile of the shift?

## Framework 5: Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)

**Use when**: Understanding why users hire a product and what they are really trying to accomplish.

The JTBD framework asks: "When users use this product, what job are they hiring it to do?"

Structure:
- **Functional job**: The practical task ("help me track my project milestones")
- **Emotional job**: The feeling they want ("feel in control and not miss anything")
- **Social job**: How they want to be perceived ("look like a reliable manager to my team")

Output: For each user segment, write the JTBD in the format: "When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]."

## Process

1. Identify which framework(s) are relevant to the question
2. Complete the full analysis
3. Synthesize key insights and strategic implications
