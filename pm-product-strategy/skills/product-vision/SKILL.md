# Product Vision Crafter

A product vision is a vivid, emotionally resonant description of the future state you are working to create. It is not a mission statement, a feature list, or a business objective.

## What Makes a Strong Product Vision

Apply this skill to: $ARGUMENTS


A strong vision is:
- **Specific enough** to guide trade-off decisions
- **Ambitious enough** to inspire the team beyond today's roadmap
- **Human-centered** - describes the change in a user's life, not a product capability
- **Time-bounded** - implicitly or explicitly set 3-5 years out
- **Memorable** - a team member can recall it without looking it up

A weak vision is:
- Generic ("be the best platform for X")
- Feature-focused ("a product that does A, B, and C")
- Purely financial ("reach $100M ARR")
- So vague it would apply to any product

## Vision Formats

### Format 1: The Narrative Vision (recommended for teams)
A 2-3 paragraph story told from a future user's perspective. "It is 2028. Sarah is a solo consultant. She opens her laptop and..."

### Format 2: The One-Sentence Vision
For use in presentations and alignment documents. Structure: [User] can [do what differently] so that [meaningful outcome in their life].

### Format 3: The Press Release Vision (Amazon-style)
Write a fictional press release from 3-5 years in the future announcing what your product has achieved.

## Process

1. Ask for: the product, the primary user, the current state, and any existing vision language
2. Identify which format fits the use case
3. Draft the vision
4. Critique it against the "strong vision" criteria
5. Offer 2-3 alternative phrasings
